The 19th century is nearing its end. Strolling across Boston, the city presents a different picture from that of the extended land mass of the metropolis it will become by the 21st century. Over the last decade, extensive land reclamations have transformed swamps and other wetlands, and even vast parts of the ocean and the Charles River, into usable land.
