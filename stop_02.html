Andrew Square, 1939, from the Boston Pictorial Archive

You've arrived at Andrew Square, where you can see Dorchester St, Dorchester Ave, Preble St, and Boston St. leading off in different directions. It's a place bustling with activity; pedestrians are filing in and out of the little shops, and a few well-dressed men are mingling outside the Oldtimer's Club at the corner of Dorchester St and Dorchester Ave.

Dorchester Street north from Andrews Square, 1902, Wire Department photographs, Boston Landmarks Commission image collection

Occasionally, a line 10 steetcar, running between City Point and Dudley, passes by, rattling and clanking mechanically. Turning northeast, you look to the right of the curved building, down Dorchester Street. Before you, a tree-lined promenade stretching into the distance, wires above, a streetcar rolling off into the distance; and far ahead, a white steeple above the treetops.

  • Looking around you, what do you see?
  • What activities can you observe?
  • How are the people dressed? What do their cars look like?
  • What pecularities does the curved building with the Oldtimer's Club at the corner posess?
  • Closing your eyes, listen to the noise of the traffic, the bustling crowd. What kind of picture do the sounds paint in your mind?
