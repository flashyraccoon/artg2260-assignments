J. W. A. Scott, View of Roxbury, 1854

You turn around, looking back the way you came. You notice the sparsely strewn buildings; to the right of the road, two thin arms of South Bay stretch out towards it. You're now standing in the middle of South Bay, the train tracks crossing the path on which you have been walking. Towards the west, or right, you can see the idyllic suburb of Roxbury on the far side of South Bay. There, on the lush, rolling hills, a few cottages and mansions stand alone, white speckles in the green landscape.

  • Looking around you, what do you see?
  • Think about the scents of the air around you, the sounds you can hear.
  • What are the qualities of the ground beneath your feet?
  • Can you see any animals? Can you see, for example, seagulls gliding through the air above you?
