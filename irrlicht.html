Irrlicht

Irrlicht - or watch it on Youtube

About the installation:

Irrlicht (German for will-o'-the-wisp) is an interactive installation - an experience crossing the boundaries between the real and the virtual world.

Use the gamepad to explore a mysterious clearing at night; in the distance, you can see lights among the trees. As you near the source of one of the lights, you can see that it's emitted by a bottle floating in mid-air, a magical aura surrounding it.
What secrets do this place and it's artifacts hold?

Moving your avatar closer to the floating bottles in the virtual world, you will notice that, as you approach each light and it grows brighter, so does a corresponding light somewhere around you in the real world.
Looking around you'll notice that there are bottles suspended accross the room as well, one for each that can be found in the virtual world. And each one will light up when you near its twin in the virtual clearing.

My work for the installation:

When I worked on this project, I was operating largely independently. In order to execute my design idea, I enlisted help from a project mentor, who helped me programming the Arduino board I used for this project. I further was able to consult my mentor for help with writing custom scripts to be integrated in Unity 3D.

I started out sketching my vision for the interactive experience. Next, I began cresting 2D assets to use in Unity 3D. I then modelled the basic terrain of the virtual world in Unity 3D. Taking inspiration from delicate paper-models, I arranged the created 2D assets in 6-pointed stars, so that the illusion of physical pbjects was created. Finally, I texturized the surfaces. While I used a rock-texture from the Unity library, I created my own for the grassy areas. I also found a matching skybox image and bottle models in the Unity Store.

Putting it all together, I animated the floating bottles, and scripted events that would make the light-orb emitting from the floating bottles grow brighter as the avatar nears them, as well as cause the light to grow dimmer when the avatar walks away from them again. Additionally, I created atmospheric particle and light effects as well as a serene-looking pond within Unity.
Finally, I created a score using GarageBand and Audacity to underline the distinct atmosphere of the virtual environment.

Moving on to the real-world components, I crafted a system of wiring, Arduino board, switchboard, LEDs and green glass bottles. When it came to setting up the installation, i suspended the LED-equipped bottles from the ceiling around the room, and connected the Arduino board to the computer running the Unity App.
